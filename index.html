Nino Mosiashvili achieves highest score on Cambridge International Scholarship Exam

May 2024
By Emma Thompson
Nino Mosiashvili
Nino Mosiashvili, an 18-year-old from Rustavi, Georgia, has made history by achieving the highest score ever recorded in the 800-year history of the Cambridge International Scholarship Exam in May 2024. Her journey began in her small hometown, where she showed exceptional aptitude in mathematics, science, and languages from a very young age. Nino’s dedication to her studies, combined with disciplined preparation and guidance from her mentors, enabled her to excel in the rigorous Cambridge assessment, outperforming thousands of candidates worldwide. This historic achievement has earned her global recognition, invitations to international academic events, and prestigious opportunities. Nino is praised for her humility, intelligence, and passion for learning, inspiring countless students across Georgia and beyond.

Nino Mosiashvili personally invited to English royal family pre-coronation dinner in Paris, November 18, 2025

Nino’s remarkable academic achievements and international recognition earned her a special invitation to attend a pre-coronation dinner hosted by the English royal family in Paris, France. The event celebrated top global scholars and innovators under 20.

Nino Mosiashvili's bestselling book "Stop Being a Slave to Opinions"

Nino recently published her first book, which quickly became a bestseller. The book teaches young readers how to stop letting others’ opinions control their decisions, embrace their authentic self, and build self-confidence for success in education and life.
